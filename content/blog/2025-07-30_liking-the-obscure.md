Some people like obscure topics/activities - sometimes just for its own sake, sometimes the obscurity itself is part of the appeal. "Obscure"? Anything that is ignored or scorned by mainstream community: unusual subgenres of music (or cinema), writing with ink on paper, cheese-making, learning an uncommon language (programming language or natural language), . This is a large part of what we talk of as being a nerd or geek and has, at least in the west that I can comment about with some knowledge, become vastly more acceptable/admirable over the last few decades.

I've come to think that my life is more interesting to the extent that I surround myself with people _who like people who_ take an Interest in the Obscure. Nerd/Geek?

* Doesn't have to be very obscure - certainly not going to be competitive about it[^competitive] - just
* Doesn't have to be an extreme or obsessive interest.

[^competitive]: As a general rule, I am, like, _the least_ competitive person. Furthermore, I'm philosophically opposed to the imposition of non-consensual competition.
