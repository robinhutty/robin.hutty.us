In the modern world's tech companies, individual employees (and, sometimes to a lesser extent, teams) are usually required[^required] to demonstrate their value proposition: the employee's Annual Review (often tied to compensation review, but that's another issue), the Manager or departmental Director who has to justify their budget/headcount request, even the initial interview/hiring process, etc. The regularity and frequency varies quite a bit depending on various factors, including but not limited to: justify _to whom_?, the kind of role, "seniority", the kind of organization, etc.

[^required]: This post is _not_ going address either the ethics of these requirements or attempt to make claims about whether this is an effective practice for the business.

Scope
: An individual, small team, or small-ish project. Basically the assessor is trying to answer questions like "Hire/no-hire (at this level of compensation)?", "Keep/disband this team?", "Is it worth pursuing this Project/Epic/ticket?".

In order to simplify, this post will assume that the "assessor" and the "demonstrator" both have the same goal: to accurately assess the value. This, of course, is almost never true - in almost all relevant situations the activity is at least somewhat adversarial. I will, with no evidence other than personal/anecdotal, claim that to believe otherwise is incorrect to the point of naivety. However, I also believe that I might be able to learn something by ignoring [Goodhart][goodhart] and exploring this topic with the almost certainly somewhat incorrect assumption that our hypothetical parties are making a _bona fide_ attempt to gain an accurate understanding of value.
