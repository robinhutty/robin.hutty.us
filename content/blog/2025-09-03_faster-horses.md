Henry Ford is commonly quoted[^misquote] as having said:

> “If I had asked people what they wanted, they would have said 'faster horses'.”

[^misquote]: There's controversy over whether he actually said it, and it seems likely to me that he did not.

Generally I've found that those who quote it are usually taking a position something along the lines of:

* If we ask [existing users] what they want, they will not imagine/consider a fundamentally different approach
* That the existing users will claim they want the same thing they already know but just that it should be better on some axis they care about such as faster/stronger/cheaper/etc.
* That the existing users do not know what they really want.
* That we should not let this claim deter us from taking a fundamentally different approach.

I think that this position is sometimes useful; that we should encourage significantly different approaches, that we should not _prevent_ such attempts. Otherwise, we would "languish" at local maxima.
