I get it - hiring is an important and difficult activity. I've been on both sides of the table many times and I've been an "interview advocate" for many friends/acquaintances. I've worked with/for many different kinds of organizations (think: .com, .edu, .org) of many different sizes, large, small, and in-between. And I still think that a lot of luck is involved - both for the potential hiring organization and the interview candidate.

## Basics

Here's what _I_ want to hear in the first message from a recruiter

* The name of the hiring company (for large companies, include the group/product/team). Seems obvious to me, but I get _lots_ of pings without it, and not just from bots. If I'm going to consider working for some org/company, I want to be able to learn about them out-of-band.
* Remote: is the role going to be fully remote or some sort of hybrid? If so, what? (and if you're open to negotiation on this, say so). Explain the current habit of the org/immediate team. While I won't go to an office in person for my day-to-day, I am quite willing to attend in-person events or team sessions occasionally.
* _Something_ about compensation expectations. Nobody, neither hirer nor potential hiree, benefits from getting on into the process only to discover that expectations are completely different, on one side or another. (More on [compensation](#compensation) below)

### Compensation

The goal here is to ensure that expectations are _similar_ on both sides of the table.

* Since most job reqs are "compensation dependent on (experience/location/etc.)", it's unlikely that you can be exact, but mention a range (and make sure that the range is not too large such as 50% of the salary)

* If your total compensation package is complex, perhaps only hit the most significant aspects in the first message.
